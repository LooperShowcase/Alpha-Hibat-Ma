What makes a great volleyball ball?

It’s easy to see why volleyball is becoming one of the most popular sports in the world. High speed spikes, big bodied blocks, and players diving all over the court make it incredibly exciting to witness. With that being said, it’s important to remember what makes it all possible.

The volleyball ball is an integral part of the game that we all know and love. It’s specifically designed for the perfect amount of velocity and bounce while being padded enough to not become a danger to players. Have you ever tried to play volleyball with a soccer ball? It hurts!

So what makes for a great volleyball? The three most important factors that determine the quality of a volleyball ball are it’s measurements, outer shell, and bladder.

  • Volleyball Ball Measurements -

    Typically, an indoor volleyball ball has a circumference of 25.5-26.5 inches, an inside pressure of 4.26-4.61 psi, and a weight of 9.2-9.9 ounces. This may vary depending on a player’s age, gender, and skill level.

  • Volleyball Ball Outer Shell -

    The outer shell of a volleyball ball is incredibly important. Not only does it impact things like grip and cushion, but it can also alter the volleyball’s flightpath.

  • Volleyball Ball Bladder-

    The bladder of a volleyball ball is the inside that allows the ball to be sfilled with air. Without a proper bladder, a volleyball ball will have inconsistent “bounce” and therefore become hard to control.
